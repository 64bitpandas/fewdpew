Broken Promises

Guilt can stem from small things. Have you ever promised to do something and never did it? Said no to helping your parents and having to watch them do it themselves? Even though these events are insignificant and carry little consequence, they can stay in our memories for years, haunting us at the most random times.
Humans are imperfect beings, and every one of us has had several "guilty moments" such as these. What are yours?

Survival

Guilt arises in plenty of situations where someone hasn't even done anything wrong. For example, Art is guilty because he didn't have to live throught what his parents had to. He feels as if it's his fault, even though he has nothing to do with it.

Death

When a loved one dies, we think back to the past, and to all the experiences we have had with them. However, what jumps out is how we have treated them badly- "I should have loved them more" or "Their death was my fault" often arise. Even though this may not necessarily be true, we still feel guilty nonetheless.

Snacks!

You've probably heard of "guilt free" snacks- the (maybe not so) healthy alternatives to regular snacks such as potato chips, cookies, and chocolates.
I've always had a question about these, though; why do we even feel guilt when eating regular snacks? We've all had our moments, however, when feeling like we are cheating ourselves by not exercising or eating healthier after eating something unhealthy. This just goes to prove that we often create unnecessary guilt in our daily lives.
(side note- why do we feel guilty about snacking but not for eating dead animals?)

No matter what happens, we feel responsible.
