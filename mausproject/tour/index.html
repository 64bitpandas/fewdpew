"Clueyness"

Blog writer Tim Urban describes one of his dad's childhood experiences, where he and his siblings ignored his father's request to play Clue:

"At the time, my dad didn’t think much of it—pretty normal day in their lives. But later on, he found himself remembering that day, and he always felt bad about it. He pictured his father sitting there at the table, now alone, with all the cards and pieces laid out. He pictured him waiting for a little while before accepting that it wasn’t gonna happen today..."

While events such as this happen every day, some create a form of guilt that create long-term memories- not happy ones- but sad, guilty ones.
Tim calls this feeling "Clueyness" in tribute to his dad's memory, and this happens to everyone, everywhere.

No matter what happens, we feel responsible.
